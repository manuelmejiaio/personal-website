For me, one of the small hacks to keep me posted with the tech/tools that I love is to follow the contributors or creators on Twitter. I like to read about what they're thinking, what they're working on and what are the next improvements for the tools. I even like to see their favorite memes 😆. So I encourage you to start this 2020 with the right foot following some brilliant people that are contributing to the Vue.js ecosystem in different ways:  
  
  
**Evan You**  
**twitter: [@youyuxi](https://twitter.com/youyuxi)**  
  
Yeah, let's start with the creator. Evan has been working with **Vue.js** since 2014, and right now he's the biggest contributor, dedicating all of his time to the framework. On his Twitter account, you can find thoughts about the next steps of Vue.js and sometimes you can find tweets about UFC 🥊.  
  
  
**Eduardo San Martin Morote**  
**twitter: [@posva](https://twitter.com/posva)**  
  
Eduardo is part of the Vue Core Team, he's focused on the **[vue-router](https://github.com/vuejs/vue-router).** Also, Eduardo is the creator of **[vuefire](https://github.com/vuejs/vuefire)**, a solution to create real-time bindings between Firebase and your Vue application. He tweets really interesting info about vue/vue-router, next conferences and a lot of memes, really funny memes 😁.  
  
  
**John Leider**  
**twitter: [@zeroskillz](https://twitter.com/zeroskillz)**  
  
John Leider is the creator of [Vuetify](https://vuetifyjs.com/), the most used Vue UI Library. On John's twitter, you can find updates about Vuetify and the next conferences where he will be participating ✨.  
  
  
**Filip Rakowski**  
**twitter: [@filrakowski](https://twitter.com/filrakowski)**  
  
Filip is the co-founder of [VueStorefront](https://www.vuestorefront.io/), a headless PWA frontend for eCommerce and the [StorefrontUI](https://www.storefrontui.io/) author, a UI framework. He writes really useful posts about performance and best practices on [vueschool.io/articles/](https://vueschool.io/articles/). Stay tuned with his latest tweets 🛒.  
  
  
**Chris Fritz**  
**twitter:** [**@chrisvfritz**](https://twitter.com/chrisvfritz)  
  
Vue consultant, and member of the Vue team. Chris helps a lot with the vue documentation on [vuejs.org](http://vuejs.org/), also, he loves to share tips about Vue app at a large scale  ([vue-enterprise-boilerplate](https://github.com/chrisvfritz/vue-enterprise-boilerplate)) 📄.  

  
  

**Jen Looper**  
**twitter: [@jenlooper](https://twitter.com/jenlooper)**  
  
Jen is a developer advocate at Microsoft. In 2018, Jen created [VueVixens.org](http://vuevixens.org/)[](https://www.blogger.com/), a cool community to teach Vue.js to under-represented people. Go and follow Jen to know more about this wonderful community 🦊.  
  
  
**Guillaume Chau**  
**twitter: [@Akryum](https://twitter.com/Akryum)**  
  
Member of the Vue Core Team, he focuses on [vue-devtools](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/vuejs-devtools/nhdogjmejiglipccpnnnanhbledajbpd?hl=en), [vue-cli](https://cli.vuejs.org/), and [vue-apollo](https://github.com/vuejs/vue-apollo), tools that will make you work like a wizard. On his twitter account, you can find information about the latest vue tool updates, next conferences, and other interesting things 🧙.  
  
  
**Alex Kyriakidis**  
**twitter: [@hootlex](https://twitter.com/hootlex)**  

  

Teacher and co-founder of [Vue School](https://vueschool.io/), one of the biggest Vue learning platforms, also the author of [The Majesty of VueJS](https://leanpub.com/vuejs2). Follow him to know more about the latest courses and I promise you will learn a lot 👨‍🏫.

  
  
**Sébastien Chopin**  
**twitter: [@Atinux](https://twitter.com/Atinux)**  
  
Creator of the famous web framework for building Vue.js apps, [Nuxt.js](http://nuxt.js/). Follow him to stay tuned with the latest Nuxt updates and conferences 💡.  
  
  
**Sarah Drasner**  
**twitter: [@sarah\_edo](https://twitter.com/sarah_edo)**  
  
Head of DX  at Netlify and a mber of the Vue Core Team. Sarah has helped with the documentation on [vuejs.org](http://vuejs.org/) and with the [vue-vscode-snippets](https://github.com/sdras/vue-vscode-snippets) which is widely used by developers working with Vue.js on VS Code. Do you like CSS animations? well, she has some incredible projects on [codepen.io](https://codepen.io/sdras/pens/popular) 🌟.  
  
  
**Michael Thiessen**  
**twitter: [@MichaelThiessen](https://twitter.com/MichaelThiessen)**  
  
Michael is a developer working on [Vidyard](https://www.vidyard.com/video-demo/?utm_source=twitter-organic&utm_medium=social&submitted=true), a video platform created with Vue.js. On his Twitter account, he shares the journey of this startup, tips for creating reusable components, and other interesting stuff. Don't forget to check his blog [michaelnthiessen.com](https://michaelnthiessen.com/) 📺.  
  
  
**Maria Lamardo**  
**twitter: [@MariaLamardo](https://twitter.com/MariaLamardo)**  
  
Front End Engineer at [Pendo](https://www.pendo.io/), also a member of  [VueVixens.org](http://vuevixens.org/). She is focused on sharing knowledge about Accessibility (a11y). Follow her to know more about the next conferences and meetups ♿.
